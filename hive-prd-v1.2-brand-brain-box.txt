WHAT'S CHANGING
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

The original onboarding flow (M-01, M-02) described clients filling in 
a brand brief manually and picking a plan. That flow is being replaced 
and significantly expanded. The new centrepiece of the client onboarding 
experience is the Brand Brain BOX — a guided setup that turns a 
business's existing web presence (or a manual upload) into a complete, 
structured brand identity that lives at the core of everything the 
platform does for them.

The simple version: Instead of asking the client to fill in a form,
The Hive now reads their existing brand from the web — or helps them 
build one from scratch — and generates a structured Brand DNA that 
powers every piece of content created on the platform, now and going 
forward. Once their Brand DNA exists, they land on a dashboard built 
around that brand, with every tool they need within reach.

05-A   THE UPDATED CLIENT JOURNEY — Step by Step
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Here is what a client now experiences from the moment they sign up to 
the moment they are on their brand dashboard, ready to create and 
approve content.

WHAT THE CLIENT DOES

1  Signs up and creates an account

   The client lands on The Hive and registers with their email. They 
   fill in basic info — name, business email, password. This is the 
   entry point — clean, fast, and welcoming. Nothing about their brand 
   is asked yet. That comes next.

2  Picks a plan that fits their budget and goals

   They choose from Takeout Media's service packages — each showing 
   clearly what is included (number of posts, platforms, asset storage 
   limit, generation credits, reports, etc.) and the price. The plan 
   they select determines their Brand Storage Limit and the number of 
   AI generation credits available to them. They pay or start a trial.

3  Creates their Brand Brain BOX

   Immediately after plan selection, the client is prompted to set up 
   their Brand Brain BOX. This is not optional — it is the foundation 
   every other feature is built on. The platform presents two clear 
   paths side by side:

   PATH A — Import from the web (recommended)
   The client pastes a link to their website, Instagram, LinkedIn, or 
   any other public-facing brand presence. The platform reads it, 
   extracts every available brand signal, and auto-generates a full 
   Brand DNA. No manual entry required.

   PATH B — Build it manually
   The client builds their brand identity from scratch, step by step, 
   directly inside the platform. They can upload their existing logo 
   or use the built-in AI Logo Maker to create one. Either way, they 
   then confirm their company name and description, and the platform 
   auto-generates the rest of the Brand DNA from those inputs.

   Both paths end in exactly the same place: a fully generated Brand DNA 
   Summary for the client to review. The import path simply gets there 
   faster because there is more to work with.

4  Reviews and confirms their Brand DNA

   The platform presents the auto-generated Brand DNA as a structured 
   summary screen. The client reads through it and can immediately click 
   into any individual section — brand description, slogan, tone of 
   voice, colour palette, or anything else — and edit it inline. Nothing 
   is locked. Once they are satisfied, they click Save Brand DNA. That 
   is the moment the brand officially exists on the platform.

5  Lands on their Brand Dashboard

   After saving, the client lands on their Brand Dashboard for the first 
   time. This is their home for that specific brand. Every tool, piece of 
   content, and asset for that brand lives here. If they have multiple 
   brands, each one has its own independent dashboard and they can switch 
   between them from the top navigation.

6  Creates content, reviews approvals, and manages their brand

   From the dashboard, the client can immediately begin generating social 
   media posts, mockups, and other marketing materials. When the Takeout 
   Media team uploads content for their review, it appears in the 
   Approvals section. Analytics, calendar, and team management all sit 
   within the same environment.

M-02 (REVISED)   Brand Brain BOX — Setup & DNA Generation
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

The Brand Brain BOX is the intelligence layer of The Hive. It is the 
first thing a client builds after signing up, and it is what every 
downstream feature — content generation, mockups, approval previews, 
and the Takeout team's creative brief — draws from. Getting this right 
matters enormously. The setup flow is designed to make it feel 
effortless.

There are two ways to create a Brand Brain BOX.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
PATH A   Import from Website or Social Media
──────────────────────────────────────────────

The client provides a URL — their website, their Instagram profile, 
their LinkedIn company page, or any other publicly accessible brand 
presence. The platform does the rest.

What happens next:

- The platform reads the provided URL and extracts all available 
  brand signals: logo, colour palette, typography, company name, 
  description, tagline or slogan, tone of voice, product or service 
  categories, and any stated mission, vision, or values
- Where signals are clearly present, the platform captures them 
  directly
- Where signals are implied or partial, the platform infers and 
  completes them
- The full output is assembled into a structured Brand DNA and 
  presented to the client for review on the Brand DNA Summary Screen

Key rules: The client can paste multiple URLs — for example, both 
their website and their Instagram — to give the platform more to 
work with. The more it can read, the more complete the Brand DNA 
will be. The client is never shown the extraction process itself — 
only the finished result, ready for their review.

──────────────────────────────────────────────
PATH B   Manual Setup
──────────────────────────────────────────────

The client builds their brand identity step by step inside the 
platform. This path is designed for clients who do not have a 
website yet, whose web presence does not reflect their current 
brand, or who simply prefer to start fresh.

The manual path has two sub-routes for the logo step:

SUB-ROUTE B1 — Upload an existing logo
The client uploads their logo file (PNG, SVG, or JPG). The platform 
reads the logo's colour profile to begin building the brand colour 
palette. The client then confirms their company name and writes a 
short company description. From those three inputs — logo, name, 
description — the platform auto-generates the Brand DNA.

SUB-ROUTE B2 — Create a logo with the AI Logo Maker
The client does not have a logo yet. They are taken into the 
built-in AI Logo Maker where they generate one. Once they are 
satisfied with their logo, they confirm it and return to the main 
onboarding flow — at exactly the same step as B1. They then provide 
their company name and description, and the platform auto-generates 
the Brand DNA the same way.

Key rule: The AI Logo Maker is a self-contained step. When the 
client finishes, they return to the onboarding flow automatically — 
they do not have to find their way back. The experience should feel 
like a single continuous journey, not a detour.

M-02A   Brand DNA — What Gets Generated
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Whether the client imported from the web or built manually, the 
platform generates the same structured Brand DNA. These are the fields 
that make up a complete Brand DNA record:

IDENTITY
- Company name
- Industry / category
- Company logo (primary, with extracted variations where available)
- Website URL (if provided)
- Connected social accounts (if provided)

BRAND COPY
- Company description (2–3 sentences, usable as an "About" block)
- Tagline / slogan
- Mission statement (what the business does and for whom)
- Vision statement (where the business is going)
- Core values (up to five, each with a short label and explanation)

VOICE & POSITIONING
- Tone of voice (e.g. bold and direct, warm and conversational, 
  professional and authoritative) — expressed as a short description 
  plus three to five tone keywords
- Primary audience (who the brand is speaking to)
- Unique selling point (what makes this brand different)
- Primary call to action (e.g. "Book a call", "Shop now", "Get a quote")

VISUAL IDENTITY
- Primary brand colour(s) with hex codes
- Secondary / accent colour(s) with hex codes
- Detected or suggested font pairing (display font + body font)
- Visual style description (e.g. minimalist, bold and graphic, 
  editorial, premium, playful)

CONTENT GUIDELINES
- Topics the brand should talk about (content pillars)
- Topics the brand should avoid
- Posting platforms (which social platforms this brand is active on)

Key rule: Every field in the Brand DNA can be edited by the client 
at any time — both during the initial review and later from the 
Brand DNA section of their dashboard. The DNA is never locked.

M-02B   Brand DNA Summary Screen — Review & Confirm
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Once the Brand DNA has been generated — through either path — the 
client sees a single summary screen that presents all generated fields 
in a clean, structured layout. This screen is the client's first 
impression of their brand as The Hive understands it. It needs to 
feel polished and credible, not like a rough draft.

What the client can do on this screen:

- Read through each section of the Brand DNA at a glance
- Click directly into any individual field or section to edit it 
  inline — no separate edit mode, no navigating away
- See their logo, colour palette, and font pairing rendered visually 
  — not just listed as values
- Regenerate any individual section if they want a different 
  AI-suggested version (without affecting the rest of the DNA)
- Add any field that was not picked up during generation
- Delete any field they do not want

Once they are satisfied with the full Brand DNA, they click 
Save Brand DNA. This action creates the brand record, closes the 
setup flow, and takes them directly to their Brand Dashboard.

Key rule: The client should never feel like they are submitting 
something permanent and inflexible. Every element is editable, both 
now and at any point in the future. The Save button confirms that 
they are happy to proceed — not that the DNA is set in stone.

M-05A (NEW)   Brand Dashboard — The Client's Home
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

After saving their Brand DNA, the client lands on their Brand 
Dashboard. This is what they see every time they log in. It is 
organised around the brand they are currently viewing — not around 
the platform in the abstract.

The dashboard home screen shows:

- A summary of what they can do: Create content, approve posts from 
  Takeout Media, manage assets, use creative tools, and access their 
  Brand Space
- Quick-action cards for the most common tasks: Create a Social Media 
  Post, Create a Mockup, Approvals Waiting, Upload to Assets
- A count of any pending approvals from the Takeout Media team — with 
  a direct link to review them
- Recent activity for this brand: last content generated, last 
  approval action taken, last asset uploaded
- Brand storage usage — how much of their plan's storage limit is 
  currently in use, shown as a simple progress indicator
- If the client manages more than one brand, a brand switcher in the 
  top navigation lets them move between brands without leaving the 
  dashboard environment

Design principle: The Brand Dashboard should feel like a command 
centre built specifically for this brand. Every label, quick action, 
and data point shown is in the context of the brand currently 
selected. Nothing generic. Nothing that belongs to a different brand.

M-05B (NEW)   Brand Navigation — Menu Structure
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Every brand has its own persistent navigation bar. This menu is 
always scoped to the brand currently being viewed. The menu items 
are:

Dashboard      The brand's home screen — summary, quick actions, 
               recent activity, and storage usage

Brand DNA      The full Brand DNA record for this brand. The client 
               can view, edit, and update any field at any time. 
               Changes here flow through to all future content 
               generation automatically.

Assets         The brand's media library — organised by category. 
               Full detail in M-06A below.

Tools          The creative generation suite. Social media post 
               generator, mockup generator (billboards, apparel, 
               models, physical media). Full detail in M-13 below.

Brand Space    A dedicated area for everything related to how this 
               brand presents itself to the world — not content for 
               social posting, but brand-level documents and 
               references. Pitch deck, company profile, style 
               guidelines, and similar materials live here.

Approvals      All content submitted by the Takeout Media team for 
               this brand, waiting for the client's review. The 
               Client Admin is the only person who can approve. Full 
               approval flow unchanged from the original PRD (M-07).

Team           Manage who has access to this brand's workspace. 
               Client Admin can invite team members and set their 
               permission level (view only, comment only).

Content        The full history of all content created for and by 
               this brand — generated content, Takeout-submitted 
               content, and published posts — in one place.

Calendar       Visual scheduling view showing what is planned and 
               what has gone live for this brand. Client sees 
               approved and published content only — never internal 
               drafts from the Takeout team.

Analytics      Performance data for this brand's published content. 
               Follower growth, reach, impressions, engagement rate, 
               and best-performing posts, updated automatically.

Key rule: This navigation is always brand-scoped. If the client 
switches to a different brand, the same menu items appear but all 
the data inside them belongs to the newly selected brand. Nothing 
from Brand A leaks into Brand B's view.

M-06A (NEW)   Assets — Brand Media Library
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

The Assets section is the brand's media library. It is where the 
client stores every visual and document resource that belongs to this 
brand. The Takeout team also draws from this library when creating 
content — having good assets here means better output.

Assets are organised into fixed categories. Within each category, 
the client can upload, view, download, rename, and delete files.

ASSET CATEGORIES

Logos          All variations of the brand's logo — primary, 
               reversed, icon-only, black, white, full colour. 
               Accepted formats: PNG, SVG, PDF.

Images         Photography, product shots, lifestyle images, 
               and any other brand-owned image assets. 
               Accepted formats: JPG, PNG, WEBP.

Models         Photos featuring real people — staff, brand 
               ambassadors, models used in campaigns. 
               Accepted formats: JPG, PNG.

Social Designs Previously created social media design files — 
               templates, completed posts, design system 
               exports. Accepted formats: JPG, PNG, PDF.

Videos         Brand video assets — brand films, product 
               videos, testimonials, raw footage. 
               Accepted format: MP4, MOV.

Directions     Creative direction documents — moodboards, 
               visual references, style direction notes the 
               Takeout team should follow.
               Accepted formats: PDF, JPG, PNG.

Brand Space    Pitch deck, company profile, capability statement, 
               and any other formal brand documents. 
               Accepted formats: PDF, PPTX, DOCX.

BRAND STORAGE LIMIT

Every brand has a storage limit tied to the client's plan. The 
storage limit applies per brand — not per account. A client with 
two brands has a separate storage allocation for each.

Storage usage is shown at all times: in the Assets section as a 
detailed breakdown by category, and on the Brand Dashboard as a 
simple summary bar. When a brand approaches its storage limit, 
the client is notified in advance and prompted to upgrade their 
plan or remove unused files.

Content uploaded by the Takeout Media team on behalf of the client 
does not count against the client's Brand Storage Limit.

Key rule: Storage is a plan feature, not a technical afterthought. 
The limit should be clearly communicated at plan selection, clearly 
visible once the client is inside the platform, and never a 
surprise.

M-13 (NEW)   Tools — Content Generation Suite
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

The Tools section is where the client generates marketing materials 
using their Brand DNA. Because the Brand DNA is already set up, every 
generation is brand-aware by default — the right colours, the right 
tone, the right visual style. The client does not need to describe 
their brand each time they create something.

Phase 1 includes two generation types:

SOCIAL MEDIA POSTS
The client selects a platform (Instagram, LinkedIn, Facebook, X), 
chooses a content type (single image post, carousel, story), and 
either writes a prompt or selects from suggested content ideas. The 
platform generates the post using the brand's DNA — colours, fonts, 
tone of voice, and messaging guidelines — and presents the output 
as an editable preview. The client can regenerate, adjust the prompt, 
or edit the result directly.

MOCKUPS
The client selects a mockup category and the platform places their 
brand — logo, colours, key messaging — onto the selected template. 
Phase 1 mockup categories are:

- Billboard (outdoor advertising placement)
- T-shirt (front and back apparel placement)
- Caps (embroidered or printed cap placement)
- Models (lifestyle mockup with a brand-dressed model)

The output is a high-resolution branded image the client can 
download, share, or save to their Assets library.

GENERATION CREDITS

Each client has a generation credit balance tied to their plan. Every 
social post or mockup generated uses one credit. The client's 
remaining credits are shown clearly in the Tools section at all times.

In Phase 1, every client receives a free generation allowance as part 
of their plan. When the free allowance is used, the client can 
purchase additional credits or upgrade their plan. Unused free credits 
do not roll over between billing periods.

Key rule: Generation is always brand-aware. The platform should 
never ask the client to describe their brand during a generation 
session — that is exactly what the Brand DNA is for. If the Brand 
DNA changes, future generations automatically reflect the update.

UPDATED BUILD ORDER — Phase 1
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

The goal of Phase 1 is unchanged: run one complete client from sign-up 
to published content. The Brand Brain BOX is now part of that critical 
path. Everything in this list must work end to end before launch.

  Client sign-up and plan selection
  Brand Brain BOX — Import path (website or social URL)
